LEGO Media International, which has its worldwide headquarters in London, was set up in 1996 to develop new products for children. Whether we're thinking about interactive software, TV programmes, books, magazines, films or music, children are always the starting point and the end goal of our activities.

Our objective is to build upon the natural fit between LEGO play materials and media products. Technology has already made a huge impact on the playing patterns and expectations of children. The challenge for LEGO Media International is to fire the imagination of children in new ways, expanding their sense of discovery and challenging their powers of creativity.

Here are some of the guidelines we use to help us unlock children's imaginations and satisfy their parents' wishes:

Set new quality standards

  • In our rigorous attention to product testing
  • In the helpful way we treat our customers
  • In the clarity of our packaging and print
  • In the consistency of our brand marketing

    Make children's play more constructive
  • By inspiring them to be creative
  • By encouraging social play
  • Through customised game play
  • Through a mix of structured and open-ended play
  • Through mental and physical challenge


    Just imagine

    Imagine the future of children's play. Imagine completely new media genres for children, new standards of product quality, new levels of creative potential. Imagine providing unlimited play potential for girls and boys. Imagine giving children mastery over technology. Imagine all of this within a value system that is highly regarded by parents and you have the essence of LEGO Media International - a company committed to growing with the children.
